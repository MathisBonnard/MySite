# Description

Siracusa is an ancient city known to have its city on an island called <i>Ortigia</i> amid a light blue clear water sea. This is also where Archimedes lived, you know the guy who said "Eureka" after he discovered the [Archimedes' principle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Archimedes%27_principle).

<br>

# How to get there

To go to Siracusa, you can take the bus or the train: both get you quite close to the city center.

In bus, there are departures every 30 (or 15!) minute with Interbus (I haven't checked for the others compagnies) and it cost around 6€ one way. You will need approximately 1h20 to arrive there.

You have a train every 2 hours-ish. The rides takes also 1h20 and it costs 9€.

And don't worry, you can go on the Ortigia Island by foot thanks to bridges (and because it's almost not an island)

<br>

# What to see and visit

Ancient city means obviously ruins to visit. I advise you to take a tour at the [<i>Neapoli</i> archeological park](https://maps.app.goo.gl/6YjTCNCmXyMCJ8K87). For 14€ you get a time-travel machine that brings you to our fellow greeks(-ish) ancesters. I think it would be best to do it on the morning when you arrive and then do the 35 min walk to the city center on Ortigia Island. 

Once you're on the island, you can't miss the Apollo Temple (even though it's not really visitable). Then follow the streets as you feel them, it's not a huge place and you can't really get lost.

You probably don't want to forget going from [<i>Fonte Aretusa</i>](https://maps.app.goo.gl/Pd1M4qQR3BVkjjvQ7) to the end of the Ortigia via the promenade on the seaside.


<br>

# Where to eat

Unfortunately I can't advise you a great place to eat because of my limited knowledge. But for sure, Sicily can offer you a lot of good food in restaurant or to take away and eat while visiting!

I you want an ice cream, Don Peppinu is a "ok-for-your-money" deal (I know it because we have one also in Catania).
